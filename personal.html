About Me

Although not known for its numerous sunny days, I grew up in the beautiful Pacific Northwest in Puyallup, Washington. My passion for astronomy developed out of a curiousity for our origins and the encouragement and support I received from my parents. As an avid amateur astronomy, my trusty Meade LX10 and I have scanned the skies together since middle school. I received my B.S. in Physics from Western Washington University (WWU) in 2012 and my Ph.D. from the University of Arizona in 2018.

History

My dual interest to our cosmic origins is our historical origins here on Earth. While the history of human endeavor is fascinating in itself, I feel strongly that we have an obligation to study the people's of the past so that we may learn from them, understand them, and remember them.

I also enjoy reading ancient history and studying the world's cultures. Although I have done the most reading on the Ancient Roman and Mediterranean world, I am especially interested in the ancient cultures of the Americas.
