About Me

Although not known for its numerous sunny days, I grew up in the beautiful Northwest in Puyallup, Washington. My passion for astronomy grew out of a curiousity about how things work and the encouragement and support I received from my parents. As an avid amateur astronomy, my trusty Meade LX10 and I have scanned the skies together since middle school. I received my B.S. in Physics from Western Washington University (WWU) in 2012 before arriving in sunny Tucson.

Ancient History

I also enjoy reading ancient history and studying the world's cultures. Although I have done the most reading on the Ancient Roman and Mediterranean world, I am especially interested in the ancient cultures of the Americas. Some of my favorite people's of the past include (roughly grouped geographically and with cultural groups mixed with empires):

  • Mediterranean: Minoan, Mycenaean, Classical Greek, Byzantine, Etruscan, Roman, Carthaginian, Hittite
  • Fertile Crescent: Sumerian, Egyptian, Akkadian, Hittite, Assyrian, Phoenician
  • Central Asia: Greco-Bactria, Kushan
  • Indian: Harrapan/IVC, Mauryan, Gupta
  • North American: Hohokam, ancient Pueblo, Mississippian
  • Mesoamerican: Olmec, Zapotec, Maya, Toltec, Mexica
  • Andean: Norte Chico, Chavín, Moche, Tiwanaku, Wari, Chimú, Inka
I think all history is fascinating and each people worthy of study—any I have left out are simply ones I have yet to learn more about! Currently on my reading list are the Hittites and Kushans.
